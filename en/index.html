Baccarat is a classic casino game that is well known, and enjoyed by players across the globe. It holds appeal among high-stakes players and is widely popular in Asian markets. The game is known for its straightforward mechanics and engaging pace.

The goal is simple: predict which side, either the Player or the Banker, will have a hand total closest to 9.

To start playing, choose your bet amount and place your wager on the Player, Banker, or Tie bet spots. You can adjust your bets as long as the betting time is still open.

Once betting closes, the Dealer deals four cards in total: the 1st and 3rd cards go to the Player side, while the 2nd and 4th cards go to the Banker side. The Dealer scans the cards starting with the Player’s hand.

If either side requires a third card, the Dealer draws it according to the Third Card Rule.

When all necessary cards have been dealt, the round concludes. The side with a hand total closest to 9 wins. If both sides have the same total, the round results in a Tie.

Winning bets are highlighted, and payouts are made based on your selected bets. Your winnings will also be displayed on your screen.

Once the round ends, the cards are cleared from the table, and a new betting phase begins.

The game is played with eight standard decks of 52 cards. Only the numbers on the cards matter. The suits of the cards (hearts, spades, diamonds, and clubs), don’t make any difference.

In Baccarat, only the number value of each card matters — suits (hearts, spades, etc.) don’t affect the outcome.

The Player and Banker hands are scored by adding the values of their cards. The highest possible total is 9.

If a hand’s total goes over 9, only the last digit counts. For example, a total of 14 becomes 4.

Here are a few examples:

  • 6 + 4 = 10 → becomes 0
  • 3 + 9 = 12 → becomes 2
  • 2 + 2 + 7 = 11 → becomes 1
  • K + 5 = 5
  • A + 8 = 9
  • 7 + 7 = 14 → becomes 4

A round concludes immediately after the initial four cards are dealt without any additional cards being drawn in these 3 scenarios:

  • If either the Player or Banker hand totals 8 or 9 points from their first two cards (a "Natural hand").
  • If the Player has 6 points and the Banker has 7 points, or vice versa.
  • If both hands result in a Tie with 6, 7, 8, or 9 points.

If both hands have an initial two-card total between 0 and 7, the Third Card Rule determines whether a third card is drawn. The Player’s hand is always resolved first.

No Commission Baccarat is an alternative to traditional Baccarat where the usual 5% commission on Banker wins is removed. This variation offers faster, more straightforward payouts. In this mode, standard Banker wins pay 1:1, just like Player wins—except when the Banker wins with a total of 6.

If the Banker’s winning hand totals 6, the payout is half the wager (0.5:1).

All tables start in Regular Baccarat by default. However, you can switch to No Commission mode at any time before the betting timer ends by using the “No Comm.” toggle.

In Baccarat, trends and streaks are tracked using charts known as “roadmaps”. These roadmaps provide a pictorial record of past round outcomes and shoe statistics, offering players insights that may help in anticipating future results.

Roadmaps start to record results with the first round of a new shoe and continue until the cutting card is reached. Once the final hand of the shoe is dealt, all records are cleared, and the roadmap resets with the next shoe.

There are five traditional roadmaps used in Baccarat:

  • Big Road
  • Big Eye Boy
  • Small Road
  • Cockroach Road
  • Bead Plate

Big Road

The Big Road is the primary roadmap, from which all other derived roads are based on.

The Big Road begins with the first result of a shoe, placed in the top-left corner. Subsequent results of the same side are recorded directly below, while opposite sides initiate a new column. This creates columns of hollow circles throughout the shoe, with blue circles representing Player wins and red circles indicating Banker wins.

On the Big Road, a Tie is indicated by a green line drawn across the last result. In cases of multiple consecutive ties, a small number appears on the green line, showing the total number of ties. If a Tie occurs before any Banker or Player result is recorded, the green Tie line will be displayed in the top-left cell of the grid. Once the first Banker or Player result is known, its circle will appear in the same cell, beneath the Tie line.

When there are more than six consecutive wins for either the Banker or the Player, this sequence is referred to as "the dragon." A common Baccarat strategy involves "following the dragon," repeatedly choosing the same side until it switches.

The Derived Roads

The Big Eye Boy, Small Road, and Cockroach Road are all derived from the Big Road and illustrate various patterns within it. These "predictive roads" reveal whether patterns exist.

Each derived road uses specific symbols: empty circles for the Big Eye Boy, solid circles for the Small Road, and slashes for the Cockroach Road. Red symbols indicate predictability, while blue symbols suggest “chaos” or no discernible pattern. These colors do not represent Banker or Player outcomes.

Derived roads do not begin at the very start of the shoe. Instead, they begin with the hand immediately following the first hand in specific columns of the Big Road:

  • Big Eye Boy: Starts with the second result in the second column.
  • Small Road: Starts with the second result in the third column.
  • Cockroach Road: Starts with the second result in the fourth column.

Once a derived road has begun, a new red or blue symbol is added after every subsequent hand. Users can click or tap on any derived road to view its content in more detail.

The Bead Plate tracks every hand in a shoe in a straightforward manner. Green circles in separate cells on the Bead Plate denote ties.

Results are displayed starting from the top-left of the grid, filling vertically down each of the six cells in a column. A new column begins once the previous one is full, irrespective of the hand's outcome.

Roadmap Probing helps players in predicting the outcome of the next round. It shows the trends in the Big Road, Big Eye Boy, Small Road, Cockroach Road and Bead Plate if either a Player or a Banker result were added.

Click/tap the Player Predict or Banker Predict button to see the trends, if the next result were a Player or Banker win, respectively.

The symbols on the buttons represent what the derived roads entry would be if player or banker wins.

Shuffling:

  • Timing: Shuffling occurs immediately after a game round concludes and the cutting card is removed from the shoe.
  • Method: Cards are shuffled either by a dedicated Shuffler at a shuffling table or by the Dealer at the gaming table, adhering to established shuffling procedures.

Cutting Card and Last Hand:

  • When the cutting card appears during gameplay, it is removed.
  • The Dealer will then announce that the current hand is the final hand of the shoe.
  • Upon completion of this last hand, no further cards are dealt until either a shoe change or a shuffle procedure is initiated.
